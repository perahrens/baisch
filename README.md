# Baisch - Player Rulebook

Baisch is a tactical card battler. Build and protect your defense, plunder decks for resources, use heroes smartly, and eliminate enemy kings.

## 1. Goal

Be the last player still alive.

You are eliminated when your king card is defeated in a king assault.

## 2. Quick Start (60 seconds)

1. Protect your king with defense cards.
2. On your turn, improve your board (take/put), plunder, or attack enemies.
3. Capture cards to gain tempo. Captured cards this turn are prey cards and unlock next turn.
4. Use jokers to get heroes and swing the game.
5. Eliminate all other kings.

## 3. Table Setup

Each player starts with:
- 1 face-down king card
- 3 face-down defense cards (slots 1-3)
- a hand of cards

Global setup:
- one main deck
- one cemetery (discard)
- two picking decks in the center (for plunder)

## 4. Round and Turn Flow

At round start, players roll dice to determine order.

On your turn, you can perform actions such as:
- take defense card (from your own slot back to hand)
- put defense card (from hand into your own slot)
- plunder a picking deck
- attack an enemy defense card
- assault an enemy king (only when legal)
- use hero abilities

End your turn with Finish Turn.

## 5. Core Combat Rules

### 5.1 Attacking Defense Cards

- Choose attack cards from hand (and in some cases own defense cards via hero effects).
- Your total strength must beat the target defense strength.
- Success: you capture target card(s).
- Fail: the target remains and usually becomes revealed.

### 5.2 King Attack vs Defense (Royal Attack)

- Your king can attack an enemy defense card directly.
- Outcome is win/draw/loss based on strength comparison.

### 5.3 King Assault (Attack Enemy King)

- Attack enemy king with hand cards or your king card.
- Success: defender is eliminated.
- If you attacked with your king and fail, you can be eliminated.

### 5.4 Prey Cards

- Cards captured this turn are marked as prey.
- Prey cards are locked until your turn ends.
- They become usable hand cards on your next turn.

## 6. Plunder

Attack one of the two center picking decks:
- Success: gain all cards in that picking deck.
- Failure: the deck grows and can become a bigger future reward.

Plunder is a key way to refill hand and maintain pressure.

## 7. Heroes

You gain heroes by sacrificing jokers.

Hero draw mapping:

| Drawn Card | Hero / Result |
|---|---|
| 2 | Mercenaries |
| 3 | Marshal |
| 4 | Spy |
| 5 | Battery Tower |
| 6 | Merchant |
| 7 | Priest |
| 8 | Reservists |
| 9 | Banneret |
| 10 | Saboteurs |
| J | Fortified Tower |
| Q | Magician |
| K | Warlord |
| Ace (red) | Choose a white hero (2-7) |
| Ace (black) | Choose a black hero (8-K) |
| Joker | Free choice of any hero |

Hero summary:
- Mercenaries: temporary attack boosts
- Marshal: stronger mobilization economy
- Spy: reveal hidden enemy defenses
- Battery Tower: deny one incoming attack per turn
- Merchant: trade cards for better quality
- Priest: convert enemy hand cards of matching suit
- Reservists: king defense boost and optional attack boosts
- Banneret: broaden attack composition options
- Saboteurs: block enemy defense slots
- Fortified Tower: stack top defense cards
- Magician: replace defense cards with fresh draws
- Warlord: special king-based attack and king swap options

## 8. Win Condition

Last non-eliminated player wins.

## 9. New Player Tips

1. Keep at least one strong hidden defense while building offense.
2. Plunder early if your hand is weak.
3. Do not risk king assaults unless odds are clearly favorable.
4. Time joker sacrifices for turns where a hero power can be used immediately.
5. Track enemy hero cooldown/charges before committing attacks.
